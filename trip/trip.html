Speyside is largest and arguably the most famous of Scotland's whisky region. Speyside is arguably and driest and warmest part of Scotland. this is the reason why speyside whisky is most sweet and fruity.

TOP Things need do in Speyside

Moray Speyside is a beautiful countryside region in the north of Scotland. You’ll find it to the east of Inverness, running from the north coastline of the Moray Firth to the rugged Cairngorms in the south. Things to do in Moray include historic places, stunning viewpoints, outdoor activities and delicious food & drink.

Explore Elgin

Elgin is located on the south coast of Moray between Inverness and Aberdeen. The Soctland's oldest museum is located in Elgin. Even you don't drink Whisky you can a lot of fun in Elgin.

Long-distance trail

Speyside Moray Coast Trail is the long distance walk trail in Scotland, and it is the one of beautiful walk trail in England.

The Moray Coast Trail has around 80km. the starting point is in Forres town center and go north to River Findhorn, and follow the coast to Cullen. You would enjoy the beautiful north Scotland bay.

Duffus Castle

Duffus Castle is located on the North of Elgin, and around 30 mins driving distance.

Duffus Castle is one of Scotalnd's finest motte and bailey castles. Mottes were most built by stone and between 1100s to 1200s in Scotland. Duffus Castle was a fortress-residence for more than 500years. the castle we can see was built around 1200 -1300s It is the once of the strongest castles in Scotland.

THE MACALLAN DISTILLERY

THe last visit point is distillery.

The Macallan Estate is a world-leading hospitality destination and birthplace of every bottle of The Macallan single malt whisky since 1824.

Its location, in Scotland’s Speyside region, is like no other. The internationally acclaimed distillery, built in 2018, is an architectural masterpiece set in a 485-acre site which includes 1.8 miles of The River Spey.
